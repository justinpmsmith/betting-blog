How Does Sports Betting Work?

Before we go into how you can make money from sports betting, it’s important to discuss what sports betting actually is. Sports betting is the act of predicting the outcome of a sports event (e.g. a football match) and placing a wager on your prediction. The sports bettor will place this bet with a sportsbook, who will then set the odds based on how likely they think the outcome is.

For example if Chelsea are playing a premier league match against Fullham and the bookies think there is a high chance of Chelsea winning then they will place low odds on the bet like 1.25(sometimes expressed as a fraction 5/4). A $10 bet on Chelsea could potentially stand to get $12.50 return($2.50 proffit). Since Fullham are expected to lose the game the odds for a Fullham win will be much higher, along the lines of 3.5(or 7/2), which means a $10 bet would get you a $35 return.

Sports betting is a huge industry. The global sports betting market is worth around $500 billion every year. It’s estimated that there are around 1.79 billion sports bettors worldwide. With a half decent strategy and a statistics backed approach of identifying value bets, you'll find that there are plenty of opportunities to make money with sports betting

How To Identify the Best Value Bets

When you begin to bet on sports, you’ll want to make sure that you’re getting good value on your bets. But what does this even mean? let us explain. To start with lets take a look at the following formulas.

probability = 100/odds

value = 100/probability

To explain we will use the previous example. Lets say we have done our research and find that Chelsea have a 70% chance of winning the game against Fullham. using the above formulas we see that this bet has a a value of 1.43 . This means that if we want to bet on Chelsea winning then the odds should be at least 1.43 for the bet to be worth it. In our example the odds were only 1.2 which means that this is not a value bet and we should not place a bet.

How do we know the probability of a certain outcome?

There are a few good resources online to get the likelihood of different outcomes. footystats and infogol are two good sources for soccer stats and Official NBA Stats has reliable statistics for NBA. Similar sites exist for just about every major sport out there.

Risk Management

Risk Management is extremely important if you don't want to lose all your money ,and it takes quite a bit of discipline. Let's say you've decided that its time to start making some money on sports betting. You open an account and deposit $50. You've managed to find a decent looking value bet but how much do you bet on it? $10? Well lets say you decide to bet $10. It only takes 5 bad bets and boom... your account is wiped out.

A general rule of thumb is to never bet more than 5% of what is in your account. This of course is not the only to way profit so depending on your strategy this can vary.It is however important to have a strategy that you stick to. A bad strategy is still better than no strategy.

Remember that you are bound to lose some bets. It is important that you stick to a strategy even after losing a bet. Another quick way to lose your money is to chase lost bets by places wager on bets outside of your strategy
