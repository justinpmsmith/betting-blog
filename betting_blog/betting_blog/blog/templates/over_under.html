Under/over betting entails predicting more or less than a certain number of points scored in a game. For example, if we place a bet on under 2.5 goals then we are predicting that 2 or fewer goals will be scored in total by the end of the game. The overs/unders market is one of the more tricky ones to master but can be very lucrative if you can identify high-value bets.

In this article, we cover using an over/unders 2.5 betting strettegy that can be used to bet on football matches.

Check the data

Infogol is a great website to get the probabilities of different football game outcomes from. From their home page navigate to the matches page. From here you will see all the games that they have statistics on sorted by league and date


Once there we can click to see more information on any given game and more importantly the under overs probabilities.


The top bar shows the probabilities based on data gathered by infogol and the bottom bars show values based on bets being placed on betting exchanges. A good idea is look for the more likely outcome which in this is case is over 2.5 goals.

Now that we know that over 2.5 goals is the most likely outcome we can use the probability to calculate the value of this bet. Which probability source do we use though? Infogol or the betting exchanges? Well you can decide which source you trust more or you could take the average between the two. In this example the average is (59 + 68)/2 = 63.5%.

It’s good practice to have a threshold probability value. This means that you don’t place bets on outcomes that have a probability value of less than this threshold. A threshold value we like to use at Wisebet is 60%. The probability value that we calculated is above our threshold so this so far is an acceptable bet. The next step is to verify if this bet has value.

Determining value

Before we place any bets we first need to see if there is value in this bet. To do this we will make use of the following simple formula.

value = 100/probability

For over 2.5 goals with a probability of 63.5% we can calculate the value of this bet as follows.

Value = 100/63.5 = 1.57.

This means that the corresponding bet should have odds of at least 1.57 for it to be a value bet. We have found that the less popular leagues such as the Turkish super league or Portuguese primeira liga tend to have more value bets than very popular leagues such as the English premier league.

You should place as many value bets as you can find as this will increase you chances of making profit. Don’t forget to practice good risk management when betting or else you will definitely make a loss.

Risk Management

Risk Management is extremely important if you don't want to lose all your money ,and it takes quite a bit of discipline. Let's say you've decided that its time to start making some money on sports betting. You open an account and deposit $50. You've managed to find a decent looking value bet but how much do you bet on it? $10? Well lets say you decide to bet $10. It only takes 5 bad bets and boom... your account is wiped out.

A general rule of thumb is to never bet more than 5% of what is in your account. This of course is not the only to way profit so depending on your strategy this can vary.It is however important to have a strategy that you stick to. A bad strategy is still better than no strategy.
